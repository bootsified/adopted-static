The Adoption Process

It is easy for an expectant mother to feel confused or overwhelmed because there are so many things to consider and difficult choices to make. That's why we're here to help. We want to provide understanding about the adoption process and all the options so a birth mother can make choices that are right for her.

Step 1 Getting Started

Making an adoption plan starts with a conversation with someone who understands.

The following options may help:

  • Call, email or IM an options counselor
  • Request a packet of information

Step 2 Adoption Paperwork

There is paperwork, but we can help with that, too. The initial paperwork helps us to customize a plan that is right for each client.

Step 3 The Decision to Live At Home or On Our Campus

Gladney has a dorm that offers a supportive living environment, but many of our expectant mothers choose to live at home.

All clients receive:

  • Exceptional medical care at no cost
  • Staff attorneys to coordinate any legal work involved in the adoption
  • Counseling and emotional support
  • Educational opportunities
  • Information exchange between adoptive parents and birth parents
  • After adoption care

The Residential program also includes:

  • Career development opportunities
  • Activities such as movies, shopping trips, eating out, pool parties, cook-outs, and other special events going on in the Fort Worth/Dallas area

Step 4 Selecting Adoptive Parents

  • An expectant mother can choose adoptive parents when she is ready.
  • Each expectant mother will tell her options counselor the qualities she is searching for in adoptive parents
  • The expectant mother will review profiles of carefully screened prospective parents who meet her requests.
  • She can talk on the phone and meet with the adoptive family that she selected.

Step 5 Placement

  • Clients can sign relinquishment papers as early as 48 hours after delivery, or any time after that.
  • The child is placed with the adoptive parents.

Step 6 After Adoption

  • Receive letters and photos of the child from the adoptive parents
  • Access to caring, confidential counseling services
  • When adoptees turn 18, they can sign our Voluntary Adoption Registry, which provides a means for birth parents and adopted children to meet when there is mutual consent

Did You Know?

Over the course of a lifetime, a college graduate will earn, on average, $1 million more than a high school dropout. Over the course of his or her lifetime, a single high school dropout costs the nation approximately $260,000 in lost earnings, taxes, and productivity.

Statistics to Consider

In 2010, there were approximately 189,000 girls between ages 15-17 who became pregnant. Their pregnancy rate was 30.1 per 1,000 in 2010, reflecting a 59% decline since 1990 and an 18% decline since 2008.

In 2010, there were 232,110 pregnancies to non-Hispanic white teen girls age 15-19.

There were 184,040 pregnancies in 2010 to non-Hispanic black teen girls age 15-19.

Pregnancies to Hispanic teen girls age 15-19 totaled 183,140 in 2010.

In 2010, 60% of pregnancies to girls age 15-19 resulted in a live birth, 15% resulted in a miscarriage, and 26% resulted in an abortion.
